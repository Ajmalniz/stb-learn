Chapter 2 · Topic 4

Theory, Law & Principle

When a hypothesis survives many experiments, it graduates to a theory or even a law. These are the highest levels of scientific knowledge. This topic explains the difference — and why it matters in biology.

4.1 Scientific Theory

Theory
A well-tested and highly reliable scientific explanation of natural phenomena. It unifies many repeated observations and data collected from many experiments.

Important points about a theory:

  • A theory is NOT a guess. When ordinary people say "I have a theory", they mean a guess. In science, a theory has been tested many times and consistently holds true.
  • A theory explains WHY something happens in nature.
  • A theory can be updated if new evidence appears — science is always open to revision.
Example

Theory of Evolution (Darwin) — explains how species change over time through natural selection. It has been tested and supported by evidence from fossils, genetics, and direct observation for over 150 years.

4.2 Scientific Law

Scientific Law
A uniform or constant fact of nature — a pattern that is always true. It is virtually an irrefutable theory. A law describes WHAT happens, without necessarily explaining why.

Why does biology have fewer laws than physics?

Biology deals with living things, which are incredibly complex and varied. The puzzling nature of life means it is hard to state things that are always true without any exceptions. Physics has more laws because physical phenomena (like gravity) behave more consistently.

Key difference

Theory explains why something happens (e.g. Theory of Evolution explains why species change).
Law states what always happens (e.g. Law of Conservation of Energy states energy is never created or destroyed).

4.3 From Hypothesis to Theory to Law

The progression in science goes like this:

Stage What it is Tested? Reliability
HypothesisIntelligent guessNot yet testedLow — may be wrong
TheoryWell-tested explanationMany times, by many scientistsHigh — very reliable
LawConstant fact of natureAlways holds trueHighest — virtually irrefutable
Real life comparison

Think of it like this: a hypothesis is like a new student saying "I think I can do this job." A theory is like a worker who has been tested and proven reliable many times. A law is like a rule that has never been broken in thousands of years of history.
