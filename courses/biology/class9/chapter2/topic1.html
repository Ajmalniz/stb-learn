Chapter 2 · Topic 1

What is Biological Method?

Science is the systematic study of nature. At the heart of biology lies a special problem-solving process — the biological method. This topic explains what it is, why it matters, and what steps it involves.

1.1 What is Science?

Science is the systematic study of nature and how it affects us and our environment. It is a body of knowledge that constantly changes as we get better and more accurate tools for investigation.

Key idea

Science does not give permanent answers — it updates itself whenever better evidence appears. That is what makes it reliable.

1.2 What is the Scientific Method?

The scientific method is a series of steps followed by scientific investigators to answer specific questions about the natural world.

It is used by all scientists — biologists, chemists, physicists — to make sure their answers are logical and supported by evidence.

1.3 What is the Biological Method?

As biology is the branch of science that studies living things, it uses a version of the scientific method called the biological method.

Biological Method
The system of advancing knowledge about living things by formulating a question, collecting data through observation and experiment, and testing a hypothetical answer.

In simple words: you see a problem → you ask a question → you guess an answer → you test it → you reach a conclusion.

1.4 Steps of the Biological Method

The steps happen in this order. Each step is explained in the next topics — but here is the complete picture first:

Step What happens Malaria example
1. Biological ProblemIdentify what you want to find outWhat causes malaria?
2. ObservationCollect facts using senses or equipmentPlasmodium seen in blood of patients under microscope
3. HypothesisMake an intelligent, testable guessPlasmodium is the cause of malaria
4. ReasoningUse logic (inductive/deductive) to plan experiment"If Plasmodium causes malaria, then all patients must have it"
5. ExperimentTest the hypothesis practicallyExamine blood of 100 patients vs 100 healthy persons
6. ResultRecord what you foundPatients had Plasmodium; healthy persons did not
7. ConclusionAccept or reject the hypothesisPlasmodium IS the cause of malaria
8. Theory / LawIf hypothesis survives many tests, it becomes theory/lawGerm theory of disease
Exam tip

The correct sequence asked in MCQs: Observation → Hypothesis → Reasoning → Experiment → Result → Conclusion → Theory → Law. Learn it in this order.

1.5 Why is the Biological Method Important?

  • It ensures answers are based on evidence, not just belief or guessing.
  • Any other scientist can repeat the experiment and check the result.
  • It works for all types of biological problems — health, ecology, environment, behaviour, etc.
Real life

Before the biological method was used, people thought malaria was caused by bad air (Latin: mala aria). The method helped scientists prove it is actually caused by a tiny parasite called Plasmodium, carried by female Anopheles mosquitoes.
