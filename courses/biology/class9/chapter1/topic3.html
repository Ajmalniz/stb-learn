Chapter 1 · Topic 3

Biology with Other Sciences, Quran, Careers & Muslim Scientists

Biology does not work alone. It joins physics, chemistry, and maths. It also connects to our faith, careers in Pakistan, and the great work of Muslim scientists.

3.1 Biology is Linked with Other Sciences

Biology is a multidimensional (many-sided) subject. For example, when an animal runs, it follows laws of motion in physics. So biology is an interdisciplinary science — it is related to other sciences.

Biophysics
Physics laws and tools used to explain life processes.
Examples: Ultrasound scan in hospital; X-rays to see bones; radioactive isotopes to find age of fossils (carbon dating); sound waves in medical tests.
Biomathematics / Biometry
Maths used to collect and study data of living organisms. Very important in research.
Example: Counting how many patients recovered after a new medicine; average height of students in a class survey.
Biochemistry
Study of molecules that form living organisms or cells. Needs knowledge of biology and chemistry.
Example: How glucose is broken in cells; how proteins are built from amino acids.
Biogeography
Where different organisms live in different regions of the world.
Example: Snow leopard in mountains; mangroves only near sea in Sindh and Balochistan coast.
Bio-economics
Study of organisms important for production and money — cost and profit.
Example: Calculating profit from a poultry farm or a fish farm.

3.2 Careers in Biology

If you study biology, you can choose many careers. Your textbook mentions these:

  • Medicine and Surgery — diagnose and treat disease; surgery repairs or removes damaged organs. Example: A surgeon removes an infected appendix.
  • Agriculture — crops, fruits, vegetables, dairy. Very important for Pakistan, an agricultural country.
  • Horticulture — new varieties of plants and their products. Example: Better roses or mangoes for farmers.
  • Forestry — growing and protecting forests; home for many plants and animals.
  • Farming — fish farms, cattle farms, poultry farms using new methods for meat, milk, leather, wool.
  • Animal Husbandry — care and breeding of useful animals (part of agriculture).
  • Fisheries — more and better fish production; fish is a good protein source.
  • Biotechnology — changing genes to make useful products like insulin, growth hormones, interferon from bacteria.

3.3 Quran and Biology

Allah has given great knowledge about life in the Holy Quran. The Quran instructs us to study life. Water is very important for life — scientists link this to protoplasm, which needs water.

Surah Al-Anbiya, Ayah 30

“We made every living thing from water.”

Water is the basis of protoplasm — the living material inside cells.

Surah An-Nur, Ayah 45

Allah created every animal from water — some creep, some walk on two legs, some on four. This shows variety in animal life.

Surah Ar-Ra'd, Ayah 4

Allah describes vineyards, fields, date-palms watered with one water, and fruits that differ — showing facts about plant growth and development.

3.4 Muslim Scientists in Biology

Muslim scientists did experiments and observations from early Islamic times and helped develop biological science.

Jabir Bin Hayan (722–817 AD)
Born in Iran. Famous in chemistry but also wrote on plants and animals. Books: Al-Nabatiat (plants), Al-Haywan (animals).
Abdul Malik Asmai (741–828 AD)
Great zoologist. Wrote books on horse (Al-Kheil), camel, sheep, wild animals, and human body parts (Khalqul Insan).
Bu Ali Sina / Avicenna (980–1037 AD)
One of the greatest Muslim scientists; founder of medicine in the Islamic world. Identified diseases like tuberculosis and meningitis. Also worked in maths, astronomy, physics, paleontology, music. Famous books: Al-Qanoon, Al-Shifa.
