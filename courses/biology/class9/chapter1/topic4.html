Chapter 1 · Topic 4

Levels of Organisation in the Living World

Life is built step by step — from tiny atoms to the whole Earth. Your textbook says all living things are made of cells, and protoplasm is the chemical basis of life.

4.1 Big Picture

Levels of organisation in the living world have a chemical foundation. Think of it like building a house: bricks → wall → room → whole house → street → city.

Key ideas from textbook
  • Protoplasm = living material inside the cell (chemical basis of life).
  • Cell = smallest unit that works as a unit of life.
  • Similar cells → tissue → organ → organ system → organism.

1. Atomic level

All matter is made of elements; each element is made of atoms (electrons, protons, neutrons).

  • More than 100 elements exist in nature.
  • 16 bio-elements are vital for life.
  • Six basic elements of life: C, H, O, N, S, P (carbon, hydrogen, oxygen, nitrogen, sulphur, phosphorus).

Example: Carbon is in sugar, proteins, and fats in your food.

2. Molecular level

Atoms join to form molecules. In cells we have bio-molecules:

  • Micro-molecules: glucose, amino acids, fatty acids (small).
  • Macro-molecules: carbohydrates, proteins, lipids (large — built from smaller units).

Example: Many amino acids join to make a protein in your muscles.

3. Cellular level (protoplasm → cell → tissues → organs → systems → organism)

  • Protoplasm: organic + some inorganic substances working together (like jelly of life).
  • Cell: when protoplasm works as one unit — basic unit of life.
  • Tissue: similar cells grouped (e.g. muscle tissue).
  • Organ: tissues arranged to do a job (e.g. heart, leaf).
  • Organ system: organs working together (e.g. digestive system).
  • Organism: all systems working as one living being (e.g. you, a frog, a mustard plant).

4. Taxonomic level — Species

Species is the smallest unit in classification. Members look similar, interbreed, and produce fertile offspring.

Example: All domestic cats belong to one species; they can breed with each other.

5. Population level

All members of one species living in a specific area = population.

Example: All parrots living on one tree = parrot population.

6. Community level

Members of different species in the same habitat = community.

Example: Parrots, crows, and sparrows on one tree = bird community.

7. Ecosystem (Ecological system)

Community + non-living environment (air, water, soil) interact. Living things depend on surroundings.

Example: You breathe in O₂ from air and give out CO₂; plants use CO₂ and release O₂ — a two-way link.

8. Biosphere level

The part of Earth where life exists = biosphere. It has many ecosystems (desert, ocean, forest, your city park).

4.3 Remember for Exams

  • Protoplasm is the chemical basis of life.
  • Smallest unit of protoplasm working as a unit = cell.
  • Organisms can be unicellular or multicellular (next topic).
  • Mustard plant = Brassica campestris (sarsoon); Frog = Rana tigrina.
