Bioenergetics & ATP

Every machine needs fuel. A car needs petrol; a mobile phone needs a battery. Living cells are no different — they need energy. This topic explains where that energy comes from, how it is transferred (oxidation-reduction), and how it is stored in the special molecule ATP.

1.1 Where Does Energy Come From?

The only source of energy on Earth is the Sun. Sunlight energy reaches the Earth and gets converted into different forms:

  • Plants convert light energy → chemical energy (stored in glucose).
  • Animals eat plants and use this energy (food energy).
  • Organisms buried for millions of years under pressure → become fossil fuels (their chemical energy preserved).
  • Sun also drives the water cycle → rivers and dams → mechanical energy → electricity.
Law of Conservation of Energy

Also known as the First Law of Thermodynamics: energy can neither be created nor destroyed — it can only change from one form to another.

1.2 What is Bioenergetics?

Bioenergetics
The study of conversion of free energy into different forms by living organisms. It deals with the energy involved in making and breaking chemical bonds in molecules of organisms.

It is the part of biology, physics and chemistry concerned with energy relationships, transformation and transmission in living organisms.

1.3 How Energy Moves — Oxidation & Reduction

In living organisms, energy is transferred through the gain or loss of electrons when chemical bonds form and break. There are two opposite chemical processes:

Process What happens Energy flow
OxidationLoss of electrons (e⁻) and protons (H⁺)Energy is released with the electrons
ReductionGain of electrons (e⁻) and protons (H⁺)Energy is stored with the gained electrons

Whenever something is oxidised, something else is reduced — they happen together as a redox reaction. Electrons carry the energy from one molecule to the other.

Real-life example

Iron rusting: iron (Fe) loses electrons to oxygen — Fe is oxidised, oxygen is reduced. The electrons carry energy from iron to oxygen.

Memory hook

OIL RIG — Oxidation Is Loss (of electrons), Reduction Is Gain.

1.4 ATP — The Energy Currency of the Cell

Just like we store electricity in batteries for later use, living cells store energy in a special molecule called ATP.

ATP (Adenosine Triphosphate)
The universal energy currency of living cells. Made of adenosine (adenine + pentose sugar) + three phosphate groups. The bonds between the phosphate groups are high-energy bonds.

Whenever an oxidation reaction releases energy in the cell:

  • An ADP (Adenosine Di-Phosphate) molecule grabs a phosphate (P) group and adds it on, becoming ATP.
  • The energy of oxidation is now stored in ATP.

When the cell needs energy:

  • ATP breaks down into ADP + P, releasing 7.3 kcal/mole of free energy.
  • The cell uses this energy for any kind of work (e.g. transporting molecules against the concentration gradient).

Equation:

ATP ⇌ ADP + P + Energy (7.3 kcal / mole)

Two opposite reactions:

  • Formation of ATP (ADP + P → ATP) — needs energy → endergonic
  • Breakdown of ATP (ATP → ADP + P) — releases energy → exergonic
Why call it "currency"?

Just like money, ATP can be used to "buy" any kind of cellular work — muscle movement, nerve impulses, building proteins, transporting molecules. Every cell deals in ATP.
