Cell Cycle & Interphase

A cell does not divide whenever it feels like it. It follows a strict sequence — grow, copy DNA, grow some more, divide. This sequence is the cell cycle. Most of the cycle is not actually division — it's the long preparation phase called interphase.

2.1 What is the Cell Cycle?

Cell Cycle
The sequence of changes that occurs in a cell between one cell division and the next.

The cell cycle has two main phases:

  • Interphase — the period of non-division (growth + DNA copying).
  • M-phase — the period of cell division (mitosis or meiosis + cytokinesis).

2.2 Interphase — The Preparation Phase

Interphase
The period of the cell cycle between two consecutive divisions. It is a period of growth and synthesis of DNA — the cell prepares itself for the M phase.

Interphase is divided into three sub-phases:

  1. G₁ phase (Gap one)
  2. S phase (Synthesis)
  3. G₂ phase (Gap two)

2.3 G₁ Phase (Gap 1)

G₁ is the period of extensive metabolic activity. The cell is preparing for DNA synthesis:

  • The cell grows in size.
  • Specific enzymes are synthesised.
  • DNA base units (nucleotides) are accumulated for the upcoming DNA synthesis.

G₀ — the "Knot" or Resting Phase

At one point during G₁, the cell may enter a phase called G₀ (G-knot) where the cell cycle stops. The cell may stay in G₀ for:

  • Days
  • Weeks
  • In some cases — the lifetime of the organism (e.g. nerve cells)
Real life example

Most of your nerve cells are stuck in G₀ — that's why it's very difficult for nerves to repair themselves after an injury.

2.4 S Phase (Synthesis)

This is the most important step of interphase — DNA replication takes place.

  • Every DNA molecule makes an exact copy of itself.
  • The chromatin material is duplicated.
  • By the end of S phase, each chromosome consists of two sister chromatids.
Why this matters

Without S phase, daughter cells would inherit only half the DNA they need. S phase ensures both daughter cells get a complete copy.

2.5 G₂ Phase (Gap 2 / Pre-Mitotic Phase)

After DNA copying is done, the cell prepares the machinery for actual division:

  • The cell grows further in size.
  • Cell organelles replicate in number (so each daughter cell will have enough organelles).
  • Enzymes required for cell division are synthesised.

Once G₂ is complete, the cell is ready to enter the M phase.
