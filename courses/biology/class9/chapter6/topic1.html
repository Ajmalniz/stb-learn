Chapter 6 · Topic 1

Metabolism & What Enzymes Are

Life is one big chemical activity — thousands of reactions happen in your body every second. But the conditions inside cells are gentle (37°C, normal pressure). So how do these reactions happen so fast? The answer is enzymes.

1.1 Metabolism — All Reactions of Life

Metabolic reactions
All the chemical reactions that take place in the body of an organism.
Metabolism
The overall phenomenon of chemical activity inside a living organism.

Metabolic reactions come in two opposite types:

Type What happens Example
Anabolism (constructive)Large molecules are formed from smaller ones — used to build cell or body structuresPhotosynthesis; protein synthesis
Catabolism (destructive)Large molecules are broken down into smaller ones — to release energy or be reused / discardedCellular respiration; digestion
Memory hook

Anabolism = Assembling (build up). Catabolism = Cutting down (break down).

1.2 The Problem — Cells Are Too "Gentle" for Normal Chemistry

Chemical reactions usually need particular conditions to happen at a proper rate — especially high temperature and high pressure. But inside a living body:

  • Human body temperature is around 37 °C.
  • Blood pressure is ~ 120/80 mm Hg.

These are far too gentle for normal chemical reactions. So how does the body still run thousands of reactions per second? It uses facilitators.

1.3 Activation Energy

Activation Energy
The minimum amount of energy needed to start (initiate) a chemical reaction.
  • If activation energy is high, the reaction is difficult to start.
  • If activation energy is low, the reaction is easy to start.
  • Example: breaking down a glucose molecule needs an initial input of energy from 2 ATP molecules.

Living bodies cannot supply huge amounts of activation energy. They need facilitators that lower the activation energy — and that is exactly what enzymes do.

1.4 What is an Enzyme?

Where the name comes from

"Enzyme" = En (inside) + zyme (yeast). The name was coined when scientists noticed that when yeast was added to fruit sap, the sap was converted into alcohol — as if something inside the yeast was driving the reaction.

Enzyme
A biocatalyst (mostly made of protein) that facilitates chemical reactions by lowering the activation energy needed to start them.

By lowering the activation energy, enzymes allow biological reactions to happen rapidly at the relatively low temperature and pressure that the body can tolerate.

1.5 Summary in One Picture

Body has gentle conditions (37 °C, normal pressure)
↓
Reactions need a lot of activation energy
↓
Enzymes lower the activation energy
↓
Reactions can now occur quickly in the body
